A Weapon System using Behavior Trees
(Based on Ramon Viladomat's GDQ Presentation - Smart Guns)

          The Smart Guns presentation focuses on increasing workflow by empowering content creators and looking for inspiration in solutions to other similar problems. There is a benefit to not having to reinvent the wheel by using a tried and trusted system.


Using Behavior Trees
          The presentation introduces the use of Behavior Trees to solve a problem of various conditionals present in their weapon design logic. With Behavior Trees, weapon logic is modularized into behavior tree tasks and presented to content creators in the form of nodes that can be mixed and matched into a weapon's behavior tree via the editor.

          Doing so adds the benefit of keeping the weapon hiearchy to a minimal size while making it more extensible, and replaces weapon states with 'Atomic' actions which the behavior tree can interrupt, cancel, or ignore as programmed.
AI Weapon Controller
          Unreal's AI only exists on the server in a Networked setting and its AI Controller manages the resources of its BrainComponent and BehaviorTreeComponent. By creating a custom AI Controller that controls Actors, an AI for Weapon Systems can possess and unpossess different weapons and load their behavior trees during runtime.

          CzarHawk's custom Hawk Weapon AI is set to work in both Standalone and Networked play and optimized for performance.


Exchangeable Tasks
          While there is a bigger takeaway from the presentation, at a glance, the modular tasks of the smart weapon system are the stars of the show. With behavior trees, the same weapon with the same projectile can be set to fire repeatedly or to charge its firepower unleashing a single deadly shot by simply changing the tasks in its behavior tree.

          These modular tasks act as tangible blocks of logic that are exchangeable, reusable, and visually manageable. And in combination with already existing behavior tree decorators, the tasks can be used to make all kinds of different weapon behavior.
